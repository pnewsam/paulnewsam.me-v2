About Me

I went to UC Berkeley for Integrative Biology and English, and came out the other end fascinated with evolutionary theory and enamored of field work. I spent the next five or seven years moving around and doing biological surveys for different outfits. I enjoyed getting to know some of the country where I'd been born.

I moved to San Francisco on a whim, and ended up working for a startup called Sourcery. I did data processing work - pretty menial stuff. But as I stayed on, my responsibilities grew, and I played a hand in developing the protocols for our growing data-processing operation. I started as the only data processor, but by the time I had left I was coordinating with an outsource team of several members, and two others in-house.

I ended up leaving Sourcery to take another couple of biology jobs, but when I was out in the field again, I realized that I missed the tech world. I wasn't sure what to do next, but I started coding, and fond that I really enjoyed it. I had to think critically and apply my creativity daily in a way that I never did when I was just collecting data in the field. So I decided to give it a go, full-time.

Project:

Ramen Ho (Prototype)

Ramen Ho! is two apps - a mobile-ready app that informs the ramen-hungry customer which ramen shops have the shortest wait times, and a tablet-ready app that allows ramen shop proprietors to be sure they're seating their customers efficiently. The restaurant-side app is a waitlist, where customers arriving at the shop can sign up to receive notifications when their table is ready. The waittimes generated from that waitlist are broadcasted to the mobile users.

Tech Stack

  • Ruby on Rails
  • Front-end: Sass, Bourbon, Neat, Font-Awesome, jQuery
  • Database: PostgreSQL
  • Authentication: Devise
  • SMS Messaging: Twilio
